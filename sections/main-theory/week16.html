Week 16: Polishing Techniques, Sustainability, and Environmental Impact

Theory Content

In Week 16, you'll focus on final polishing techniques to enhance your project's finish. Additionally, we'll discuss sustainability and explore the environmental impact of timber use, emphasising responsible woodworking practices.

Polishing Techniques

Proper polishing methods ensure a professional-quality finish:

  • Surface Preparation: Fine sanding to remove imperfections and create a smooth surface.
  • Polishing Methods: Apply polishing compounds with cloth, buffer, or machine polisher.
  • Final Inspection: Assess polished surfaces under good lighting for evenness and gloss.

Sustainability and Environmental Impact

Understand the environmental implications of timber sourcing:

  • Sustainable Forestry: Select timber certified by bodies such as FSC to ensure ethical sourcing.
  • Reducing Waste: Plan effectively to minimise waste through careful measurements and precise cutting.
  • Environmental Responsibility: Use eco-friendly products and finishes to reduce your ecological footprint.

Practical Activity

  • Polish your bedside table surfaces carefully, achieving a professional standard.
  • Research the sustainability of materials used in your project and create a brief report summarising your findings.
