Week 13: Finishing Techniques and Timber Logging

Theory Content

In Week 13, you will explore various finishing techniques crucial for enhancing the appearance, protection, and durability of your bedside table. Additionally, you'll delve into timber logging practices and sustainability considerations, gaining a deeper appreciation of your project's environmental impact.

Finishing Techniques

Applying appropriate finishes improves aesthetics and protects timber:

  • Surface Preparation: Sanding progressively finer grits ensures a smooth finish.
  • Types of Finishes: Choose from oils, varnishes, waxes, stains, or paints based on your project's requirements.
  • Application Methods: Understand and utilise brushing, spraying, or wiping techniques for even and smooth finishes.
  • Safety Considerations: Work in well-ventilated areas and wear proper PPE, such as gloves and respirators, when handling finishes.

Timber Logging and Sustainability

  • Logging Practices: Select timber from sustainably managed sources.
  • Environmental Impact: Be aware of deforestation and habitat destruction issues.
  • Certification: Recognise certification bodies like FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) for sustainable timber.

Practical Activity

  • Prepare your bedside table surfaces thoroughly and apply your chosen finish effectively.
  • Research and record the timber types used, documenting their origins and sustainability certifications.
