Bedside Table Project Unit

This is a 20 week unit that extends woodworking skills by guiding students through the design and construction of a small bedside table.

Summary & Duration

Duration: 20 Weeks (40-minute lessons, 4 per week)
Focus: Joint construction, drawer assembly, machine operation, finishing and evaluation.

Unit Overview

Students create working drawings and a cutting list, select suitable timber and construct the table using hand and power tools. Safe work habits and accurate documentation are emphasised throughout.

Learning Outcomes & Assessment

  • IND5-1: identifies, assesses, applies and manages the risks and WHS issues associated with using a range of tools, equipment, materials, processes and technologies.
  • IND5-2: applies design principles in the modification, development and production of projects.
  • IND5-3: identifies, selects and uses a range of hand and machine tools, equipment and processes to produce quality practical projects.
  • IND5-4: selects, justifies and uses a range of relevant and associated materials for specific applications.
  • IND5-5: selects, interprets and applies a range of suitable communication techniques in the development, planning, production and presentation of ideas and projects.
  • IND5-6: identifies and participates in collaborative work practices in the learning environment.
  • IND5-7: applies and transfers skills, processes and materials to a variety of contexts and projects.
  • IND5-8: evaluates products in terms of functional, economic, aesthetic and environmental qualities and quality of construction.
  • IND5-9: describes, analyses and uses a range of current, new and emerging technologies and their various applications.
  • IND5-10: describes, analyses and evaluates the impact of technology on society, the environment and cultural issues locally and globally.

Assessment: Practical – 20% | Theory – 10% (folio and written tasks)

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1: Safety Review & Design Brief

Content: Introduce project requirements and review safety rules.

Theory: WHS legislation overview and how to interpret the design brief.

Practical: Begin concept sketches.

Resources: Design brief and safety guidelines – Google Classroom Week 1.

Week 2: Working Drawings & Cutting List

Content: Produce detailed drawings and compile a cutting list.

Theory: Drawing conventions, measuring tools and accuracy.

Practical: Select timber and mark out components.

Resources: Drawing templates and cutting list sheet – Google Classroom Week 2.

Week 3: Prepare Legs and Rails

Content: Shape legs and rails; practise mortise and tenon joints.

Theory: Timber properties and joint options for small furniture.

Practical: Cut and plane pieces to size.

Resources: Mortise and tenon guide – Google Classroom Week 3.

Week 4: Assemble Table Frame

Content: Dry fit legs and rails and check for squareness.

Theory: Clamping techniques and adhesive selection.

Practical: Glue and clamp the frame.

Resources: Adhesives data sheet – Google Classroom Week 4.

Week 5: Drawer or Shelf Construction

Content: Construct drawer or shelf components and fit runners.

Theory: Drawer joinery and hardware options.

Practical: Continue assembly and adjust the fit.

Resources: Drawer plans and hardware guide – Google Classroom Week 5.

Week 6: Fit Top & Hardware

Content: Attach the top and install any hardware.

Theory: Fasteners, hinges and surface preparation for finishing.

Practical: Sand the assembled table.

Resources: Hardware specifications – Google Classroom Week 6.

Week 7: Surface Preparation

Content: Discuss finishes and surface defects.

Theory: Sanding grits and defect repair methods.

Practical: Fill imperfections and sand smooth.

Resources: Sanding technique video – Google Classroom Week 7.

Week 8: Apply Finish

Content: Apply an oil or clear coat.

Theory: Types of finishes and safe application.

Practical: Coat surfaces and allow to dry.

Resources: Finishing guide and safety sheet – Google Classroom Week 8.

Week 9: Evaluation & Folio Completion

Content: Reflect on the project process.

Theory: Evaluation criteria for finished projects.

Practical: Photograph the table and finalise the folio.

Resources: Folio checklist – Google Classroom Week 9.

Week 10: Hand In & Review

Content: Submit the project and review learning.

Theory: Project management reflection and self-assessment.

Practical: Present finished work and assist with clean up.

Resources: Submission checklist – Google Classroom Week 10.
