Weeks 1 & 2: Introduction to Workshop Safety, Marking, and Measuring

Week 1: Workshop Safety and Basic Procedures

Understanding basic workshop safety and procedures is crucial. Key points include:

  • WHS Signs: Recognise and understand standard safety signage. These signs help identify hazards, mandatory actions, and prohibited activities.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Always wear safety glasses, protective footwear, and hearing protection when required.
  • Safe Tool Use: Always report damaged or improperly functioning tools immediately. Never remove or modify safety guards.

Practical Activity

  • Familiarise yourself with workshop layout and safety equipment (fire extinguisher, first aid kit).
  • Practise accurate measuring and marking using rulers, tapes, and squares.

Week 2: Design Sketching, Measuring, and Marking

In Week 2, precision and clarity are key. Understand these concepts:

  • Design Principles: Focus on balance and proportion when sketching your designs.
  • Measuring and Marking: "Measure twice, cut once." Use precise tools like rulers, marking gauges, and squares for accuracy.

Practical Activity

  • Sketch at least two initial designs for your bedside table.
  • Practise marking out your timber accurately to ensure minimal waste.
