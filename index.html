Bedside Table Theory

Week 1: Introduction, OHS, and Risk Management

Overview

Welcome to the Bedside Table project! This project builds on skills from the Mirror project and reinforces safe workshop practices.

Workplace Health and Safety (WHS) Refresher

The NSW Work Health and Safety (WHS) Act and Regulations guide all school workshop activities. Remember to:

  • Follow workshop signage and instructions.
  • Report hazards, damaged equipment or unsafe conditions.
  • Wear correct PPE – safety glasses, ear protection, closed shoes and appropriate clothing.

Risk Management

Risk management means identifying hazards, assessing their impact and controlling or eliminating risks. Key steps include:

  1. Identify Hazards: recognise anything that could cause harm.
  2. Assess Risks: consider likelihood and severity of injury.
  3. Control Risks: use guards, PPE, training and emergency procedures.
  4. Review Controls: regularly check that controls are effective.

Week 2: Design, Sketches, Measuring, and Mark-Out

Overview

This week, you'll build on your understanding of woodworking principles by creating detailed design sketches and accurately measuring and marking out your materials. Precision at this stage ensures successful construction and minimal waste.

Design Principles

Remember, effective design incorporates:

  • Balance and Proportion: Ensure your bedside table is aesthetically pleasing and structurally sound.
  • Functionality: Consider the practical use of your table, including drawer size and shelf space.
  • Materials: Choose appropriate timber types, considering grain, durability, and appearance.

Measuring and Marking Out

Accurate measurement and marking are critical:

  • Always use sharp pencils or marking knives for clear lines.
  • Double-check all measurements before cutting ("measure twice, cut once").
  • Tools such as rulers, try squares, and marking gauges must be correctly used to ensure precision.

Practical Activity

  • Sketch your bedside table designs, showing detailed dimensions.
  • Clearly label each component.
  • Cut timber to length following accurate marking.

Week 3: Dressing and Preparing Timber - DAR and FEWTEL

Theory Content

In Week 3, your focus will shift towards accurately dressing and preparing your timber. The process is critical for achieving the precise dimensions needed for a quality final product.

Dressing Timber (DAR)

DAR stands for Dressed All Round, meaning timber is planed smooth on all sides. This step is vital for accurate joinery and assembly.

FEWTEL Method

Use the FEWTEL method to systematically prepare timber:

  1. Face: Plane one face flat.
  2. Edge: Plane one edge square to the first face.
  3. Width: Cut or plane the timber to the final width.
  4. Thickness: Thickness the timber to the final dimension.
  5. End: Trim the ends square and to the final length.
  6. Length: Confirm the timber is accurately measured to length.

Tools and Techniques

  • Hand Plane: Ideal for flattening and smoothing.
  • Thicknesser: Used for achieving uniform thickness across timber.
  • Always verify squareness with a try square.

Practical Activity

  • Use the FEWTEL method to dress your timber to precise dimensions.
  • Ensure each piece is accurately marked and checked for squareness.

Week 4: On-Guard Mortiser and Joinery Techniques

Theory Content

In Week 4, you'll enhance your woodworking skills by mastering the use of the mortiser machine and developing precise joinery techniques, specifically mortise and tenon joints. Achieving accuracy with these techniques is essential for producing durable and structurally sound furniture.

Mortiser Safety and Operation

The mortiser is a specialised woodworking machine designed for creating precise square or rectangular mortises (holes) in timber. These mortises are essential for crafting strong mortise and tenon joints.

Key safety practices include:

  • Always wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), particularly safety glasses.
  • Verify the mortise chisel and bit are sharp, correctly aligned, and securely fitted.
  • Ensure timber is firmly clamped before commencing operation.
  • Keep hands clear of the cutting area and avoid distractions while operating the machine.

Mortise and Tenon Joinery

The mortise and tenon joint is recognised for its strength and traditional value in woodworking. It involves two components:

  • Mortise: A precisely cut hole or recess in timber.
  • Tenon: A projection shaped to fit snugly into the mortise.

Accuracy in measurement and marking, along with meticulous cutting, ensures the joint’s effectiveness and strength.

Practical Activity

  • Accurately mark out and cut mortises using the mortiser.
  • Begin marking and cutting matching tenons.
  • Test-fit each joint carefully for optimal accuracy.

Week 5: Mortise and Tenon Joints & Bandsaw Safety

Theory Content

In Week 5, you will deepen your understanding of mortise and tenon joinery, utilising the bandsaw to ensure accuracy and quality in your woodworking. Precise construction techniques and thorough safety practices are paramount.

Mortise and Tenon Joints

Focus on accurately constructing mortise and tenon joints:

  • Carefully measure and mark components to ensure perfect alignment.
  • Cut tenons carefully to fit securely into mortises.
  • Aim for clean cuts to reduce additional sanding and adjustment.

Bandsaw Safety and Operation

Effective use of the bandsaw is essential for precision cutting of tenons. Always:

  • Wear proper PPE, particularly safety glasses and hearing protection.
  • Position the blade guide just above the timber to minimise blade deflection.
  • Control the feed rate to avoid binding or twisting the timber.
  • Regularly inspect and adjust blade tension and sharpness to ensure optimal performance.

Practical Activity

  • Complete mortises accurately using the mortiser.
  • Precisely cut tenons using the bandsaw.
  • Test-fit joints meticulously, making necessary fine adjustments.

Week 6: Bandsaw SOP and Refinement of Mortise and Tenon Joints

Theory Content

In Week 6, you will concentrate on Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for the bandsaw, ensuring the accurate and safe refinement of mortise and tenon joints. Mastery of these techniques is essential for high-quality joinery and woodworking excellence.

Bandsaw Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

Following correct SOP is crucial for safe, precise, and effective use of the bandsaw:

  • Pre-Use Checks:
    • Confirm blade tension and sharpness.
    • Verify safety guards and guides are correctly adjusted and securely fitted.
  • Operation Guidelines:
    • Adjust the blade guide to sit just above the timber surface.
    • Maintain consistent, moderate feeding speed to avoid blade drift.
    • Avoid pushing too hard, which could lead to blade binding or inaccurate cuts.
  • Post-Use Checks:
    • Clean the bandsaw thoroughly and inspect the blade for wear or damage.
    • Report any abnormalities or issues immediately to your teacher.

Refinement of Mortise and Tenon Joints

This stage is essential for achieving a high-quality final fit:

  • Accurately check joint alignment and make precise minor adjustments as needed.
  • Dry assemble the joints to test for perfect fitting.
  • Fine-tune tenon dimensions carefully to ensure a snug, secure fit.

Practical Activity

  • Follow the bandsaw SOP carefully to finish cutting tenons.
  • Check each mortise and tenon joint thoroughly, adjusting and refining as required.

Week 8: Dry Clamping, Work Method Statements (WMS), and SOPs

Theory Content

Week 8 focuses on essential preparation steps for project assembly, including dry clamping, writing Work Method Statements (WMS), and revisiting Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). These steps ensure a structured and safe approach to woodworking, aiding accurate assembly and high-quality outcomes.

Dry Clamping

Dry clamping involves assembling your project without glue to check:

  • Component alignment and accuracy of joints.
  • Overall structure squareness and stability.
  • Identification and correction of potential problems before final assembly.

Check squareness by:

  • Measuring diagonals; equal measurements indicate squareness.
  • Using a builder’s square for precise corner checks.

Work Method Statements (WMS)

A WMS is a detailed description of how a specific woodworking task is performed safely, efficiently, and effectively. A good WMS includes:

  • Task identification and sequence.
  • Required tools, materials, and PPE.
  • Clearly defined safety measures.

Revisiting SOPs

Ensure consistent adherence to SOPs for each tool and machine used in your project:

  • Bandsaw
  • Mortiser
  • Thicknesser

Regular SOP reviews reinforce safety protocols and operational efficiency.

Practical Activity

  • Dry assemble your bedside table, making adjustments as necessary.
  • Develop clear and detailed Work Method Statements for your project tasks.

Week 9: Adding Design Features and Orthogonal Drawings

Theory Content

In Week 9, your focus shifts towards enhancing the aesthetics and functionality of your bedside table through design features. You will also refine your skills in producing accurate and detailed orthogonal drawings using Computer-Aided Design (CAD).

Adding Design Features

Incorporating distinctive design features can significantly enhance the appearance and utility of your furniture. Consider:

  • Decorative mouldings or routed edges
  • Inlays or veneers
  • Secret drawers or hidden compartments
  • Practical hardware such as hinges, handles, and drawer runners

Always ensure these features complement the overall design and function of your bedside table.

Orthogonal Drawings with CAD

Creating accurate orthogonal drawings in CAD software helps visualise your project clearly, allowing for precise measurements and efficient material usage.

  • Accuracy: Ensure all measurements are exact and clearly labelled.
  • Clarity: Include all essential views (front, top, side).
  • Detail: Clearly depict design features, joints, and hardware placements.

Practical Activity

  • Identify suitable design features and hardware for your bedside table.
  • Develop comprehensive orthogonal drawings using CAD software, detailing chosen design features.
