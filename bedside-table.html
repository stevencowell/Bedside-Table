Students design, draw, and construct a timber bedside table over ten weeks, documenting progress and applying safe work practices.

  • Download Unit Overview (PDF)

Main Theory Content

Week 1: Workshop Safety and Project Introduction

Students review workshop rules, PPE requirements, and risk management before beginning the bedside table project.

They examine hazards in the wood shop and practice a basic risk assessment.

Week 2: Design Principles and Planning

Focus on the design brief, ideation sketches, proportion, balance, and form versus function for the table.

Week 3: Materials, Measuring, and Marking Out

Students compare timber types, select materials, and practice accurate measurement and marking techniques.

Week 4: Joinery Techniques – Mortise and Tenon Joint

Introduction to cutting and fitting mortise and tenon joints for the table frame.

Week 5: Technical Drawing – Orthographic Projection

Students produce orthographic drawings of their bedside table, learning correct view placement and dimensioning.

Week 6: Machine Tools and Safe Operating Procedures

Covers the drill press, band saw, sanders, and routers with emphasis on SOPs and safety zones.

Week 7: CAD Work – Digital Design and Documentation

Students model their design in CAD software and explore extracting orthographic views from the model.

Week 8: Assembly Techniques and Jig Usage

Dry fitting, squaring the frame, clamping methods, and how jigs aid consistent assembly.

Week 9: Surface Preparation and Finishing Techniques

Step-by-step sanding process and application of finishes such as polyurethane or oil.

Week 10: Design Detailing and Project Evaluation

Final touches like hardware installation, followed by evaluating the table against the design brief.

Support Theory Content (Optional)

Week 1: Workshop Safety Basics

Explains PPE terms and simple safety rules using clear examples.

Week 2: Simplified Design Steps

Uses checklists and templates to help students develop a basic bedside table design.

Week 3: Measuring Practice

Extra practice reading rulers and marking lines accurately on scrap timber.

Week 4: Joint Practice

Students make a sample mortise and tenon joint in scrap wood before cutting their project pieces.

Week 5: Drawing Help

Provides grid paper and tracing guides to assist with orthographic sketches.

Week 6: Machine Demos

Step-by-step demonstrations build confidence before students operate machines.

Week 7: CAD Templates

Uses prepared CAD files that students modify rather than starting from scratch.

Week 8: Assembly Checklist

A simple list reminds students to dry fit, check square, then glue and clamp.

Week 9: Finishing Tips

Shows easy ways to sand and wipe-on finish for a neat result.

Week 10: Project Reflection

Guided questions help students evaluate what they learned and how to improve.

Advanced Theory Content (Optional)

Week 1: Industry Safety & Advanced Systems

Explores professional workshop inductions and technologies like flesh-sensing saws.

Week 2: Designer's Insight

Looks at design thinking methods and sustainability considerations in furniture design.

Week 3: Precision, Jigs, and Industry Techniques

Introduces story sticks, jigs, and tolerances used by professional woodworkers.

Week 4: Advanced Joinery & Industry Links

Discusses haunched or through tenons, machinery for joinery, and quality control.

Week 5: CAD Integration & Detailed Drafting

Shows how CAD software can generate orthographic views and even 3D print models.

Week 6: Modern Woodworking Tech & Industry Practices

Covers CNC routers, dust extraction, and workshop layout in professional shops.

Week 7: Advanced CAD & Integration with Production

Encourages modeling joinery in CAD and exporting files for CNC or 3D printing.

Week 8: Professional Assembly Techniques & Jig Design

Explores assembly tables, pocket hole jigs, and clamping pressure guidelines.

Week 9: Fine Finishing & Sustainable Practices

Looks at water-based finishes, spray systems, and environmentally friendly options.

Week 10: Extension – Showcasing and Real-World Connection

Students present their finished table, consider marketability, and discuss industry trends.
