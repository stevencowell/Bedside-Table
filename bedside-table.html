  • Industrial Technology Years 7-10 outcomes (IND51–IND510).
  • Focus on safe tool use, design principles and quality production.
  • Collaborative practices and evaluation of finished products.

Assessments

Practical / Folio – 45 | Research Task – 5 | Class Test – 10

Bedside Table Unit Overview

As the culmination of the 200-hour course, this unit provides the opportunity for students to draw together all the knowledge, skills and understanding developed in the previous units. Students select and use techniques, tools and materials suited to their individual project. The development and production of the project are documented in detail and presented in a comprehensive project report using appropriate workplace communication skills.

Learning Outcomes

  • IND51 identifies, assesses, applies and manages WHS risks.
  • IND52 applies design principles in project development.
  • IND53 uses a range of hand and machine tools.
  • IND54 selects relevant materials for specific applications.
  • IND55 applies suitable communication techniques.
  • IND56 participates in collaborative work practices.
  • IND57 transfers skills and processes to various contexts.
  • IND58 evaluates products for function, economy and aesthetics.
  • IND59 analyses and uses new and emerging technologies.
  • IND510 evaluates the impact of technology on society and the environment.

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1: Introduction. OHS and Risk Management

Refresh safety procedures and begin planning.

Week 2: Design and sketches. Measure and Mark-out

Develop design ideas and mark out timber accurately.

Week 3: Dress and prepare timber to size

Use hand tools and machines to plane and size timber.

Week 4: On-guard Mortiser

Safe use of the mortising machine for joint preparation.

Week 5: Mortise and Tenon Joints. On guard - Bandsaw

Cut mortise and tenon joints and review bandsaw safety.

Week 6: Mortise and Tenon Joints - SOP Bandsaw

Continue joint work and complete SOP for bandsaw.

Week 7: On-guard SOP - Thicknesser. Orthogonal Drawings

Use the thicknesser safely and create orthogonal drawings.

Week 8: Dry Clamping. Work Method Statements and SOP’s

Practise dry clamping and develop work method statements.

Week 9: Adding Design Features. Orthogonal Drawings

Explore design features such as inlays and update drawings.

Week 10: Adding Design Features. Orthogonal Drawings

Continue refining design features and drawings.

Week 11: Assemble / Gluing. Costing

Assemble components, glue joints and calculate costs.

Week 12: Assemble / Gluing. Sequencing and Time planning

Plan assembly sequence and develop a timeline.

Week 13: Finishing Techniques. Logging

Prepare surfaces and log progress in folio.

Week 14: Finishing Techniques. Industry Visit / link

Compare school methods with industry practices.

Week 15: Safe handling of chemicals and MSDS

Learn about safe chemical use and material safety data sheets.

Week 16: Polishing. Sustainability and Environmental Impact

Apply polish and discuss environmental considerations.

Week 17: Port Folio Presentation and Assessment

Prepare final portfolio presentation for assessment.

Week 18: Class Test

Complete a theory test covering unit content.

Week 19: Catch Up

Time allocated to finish any outstanding work.

Week 20: Hand in project and folio for marking

Submit final project and maintain the workshop.
